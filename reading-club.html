Why reading matters (importance)
  • Access to knowledge: Reading grows background knowledge—fuel for comprehension across subjects.
  • Vocabulary & language: Wide reading accelerates vocabulary, syntax, and academic language.
  • Thinking skills: Texts train inference, argument, analysis of structure, and evaluating sources.
  • School & test success: Strong reading drives achievement in every subject and on exams.
  • Lifelong benefits: Reading habits correlate with better writing, empathy, and independent learning.
How we explicitly teach reading
  • I do → We do → You do: We model a skill (think-aloud), practice together, then students apply independently.
  • Foundations (K–G2): Daily phonemic awareness, systematic phonics, decodable texts for accurate decoding.
  • Fluency: Phrasing and expression with 1-minute timings (WCPM), echo/choral reading, and repeated reads.
  • Vocabulary & morphology: High-utility words, word parts (prefix/root/suffix), and usage in context.
  • Comprehension strategies: Predict–question–clarify–summarize, text structure signal words, main idea, inference.
  • Question types: Literal, inferential, and evaluative (QAR: Right There / Think & Search / Author & Me).
  • ESL scaffolds: Visuals, sentence frames, partner talk, bilingual supports, and background-knowledge boosts.
We use short, focused mini-lessons, guided practice, and immediate feedback to make skills stick.
How we explicitly teach reading comprehension
  1. Activate & prime: Build context; preview text structure; front-load critical vocabulary.
  2. Model: Think-aloud on one strategy (e.g., finding main idea using headings and topic sentences).
  3. Guided practice: Students apply with support (graphic organizers, text-dependent questions).
  4. Fluency pass: Phrased reading to free up cognitive load for meaning.
  5. Independent read & write: Stop-and-jot, annotate, or GIST summary (10–15 words) to consolidate.
  6. Discuss & evaluate: Peer share; check evidence; adjust inferences; connect ideas across texts.
  7. Assess & reteach: Exit ticket; quick re-teach for any strategy that needs another at-bat.
Club routine (weekly session)
  1. Warm-up (5′): Phonemic drill / morphology review.
  2. Mini-lesson (10′): One explicit skill (e.g., compare/contrast text structure).
  3. Guided reading (20′): Leveled groups with targeted decoding/fluency/comprehension work.
  4. Fluency timing (5′): 1-minute WCPM; phrasing practice.
  5. Vocabulary station (10′): Morphology + usage tasks.
  6. Comprehension task (10′): Organizer + text-dependent questions; short write (GIST/SWBST).
  7. Choice reading (5′): Interest-based texts to build volume.
  8. Exit ticket (3′): Quick check; next steps.
Assessment & progress tracking
  • Decoding: Pattern checks, accuracy with decodables.
  • Fluency: WCPM graphs, phrasing and expression notes.
  • Comprehension: MAZE/cloze, written responses, rubric-based summaries.
  • Reporting: Bi-weekly notes to families with tips and next targets.
