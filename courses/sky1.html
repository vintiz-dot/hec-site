Big ideas
  • Technology & Innovation
  • Nature & Adaptation
  • Leadership & Community
Essential questions
  • How can technology help people?
  • What can we learn from nature?
  • What does it take to be a leader?
Intended outcomes
  • 3‑minute presentations
  • Summaries from ~300‑word articles
  • 10–12 sentence reports with visuals
  • Modal verbs; comparatives/superlatives
Success criteria
  • Summarize into 5‑6 bullet notes
  • Present with eye contact and purposeful visuals
  • Report with clear intro/body/conclusion
Educational journey
  1. Entry: summary + speaking diagnostic sets targets.
  2. Sprint 1: research a problem; outline and write a report; revise for cohesion.
  3. Sprint 2: rehearse and deliver a 3‑minute talk; peer review using rubric.
  4. Showcase: product demo or poster session; self‑assessment + coach feedback.
  5. Next: Flyers/KET route or Writing G6 for academic polish.
How we teach
Evidence‑based practices
  • Research cycle with sources log
  • Graphic organizers → multi‑paragraph writing
  • Deliberate practice for presentations
  • Peer review against rubrics

INTASC connections (course‑specific)
  • Std 4 – Content Knowledge: Cause/effect and compare/contrast language taught with signal words and mentor texts.
  • Std 5 – Application of Content: Prototype/poster addresses a real local problem; claims supported by cited sources.
  • Std 6 – Assessment: Summary notes checked for accuracy; oral rubric covers clarity, evidence and delivery.
  • Std 7 – Planning: Two‑week sprint: research → draft → feedback → revision; checkpoints are public.
  • Std 8 – Instructional Strategies: Socratic discussion, fishbowl practice, rehearsal ladders and slide‑design mini‑lessons.
  • Std 10 – Leadership & Collaboration: Team norms and rotating facilitation build leadership and collaboration.
